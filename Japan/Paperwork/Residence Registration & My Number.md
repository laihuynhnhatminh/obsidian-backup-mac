## Priority: Do This Within 14 Days of Moving In

After arriving in Japan and finding your apartment, you must **register your address at the local ward/city office (区役所 / 市役所)**. This is the foundation of everything else — health insurance, pension, My Number, bank accounts — all require a registered address first.

---

## Step 1: Residence Registration (住民登録 / 転入届)

### Where to Go:

**Sapporo City Office (札幌市役所)** or the ward office for your specific ward (区役所). Sapporo has 10 wards (区).

### What You Need:

- [ ] Passport
- [ ] Residence Card (在留カード)
- [ ] Your new address in Japan

### What Happens:

- Your address is registered in the **Juminhyo (住民票)** — your official residence certificate
- This document is needed for almost everything: bank accounts, pension enrollment, My Number, etc.
- You can obtain copies of your 住民票 at ward offices or **convenience stores (コンビニ)** using your My Number Card

### Timeline:

Within **14 days** of moving — this is a legal requirement.

---

## Step 2: My Number (マイナンバー)

### What It Is:

A 12-digit personal identification number assigned to every resident of Japan (including foreigners staying 3+ months). Used for taxes, pensions, health insurance, and government services.

### How You Get It:

After completing residence registration, your **My Number notification (通知カード)** is mailed to your registered address within 1–2 months.

### My Number Card (マイナンバーカード) — Optional but Highly Recommended:

The physical plastic IC card with your photo. Apply at the ward office or via the Mynaportal app/website.

**Benefits of having the card:**

- Functions as **official government-issued photo ID**
- As of December 2024, integrates with your **health insurance card (Myna Insurance Card)**
- As of March 2025, can be combined with your **driver's license**
- Allows access to government services online (e-Tax, etc.)
- Can print residence certificates at convenience store kiosks (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson)

### Application:

1. Fill in the application form that comes with the notification card
2. Submit photo and form (can do at ward office or online via QR code on the form)
3. Wait 4–8 weeks, then pick up the card in person at the ward office with your ID

> 💡 Apply for this as soon as you get your notification — it takes time and you'll want it early.

## Juminhyo (住民票) — Residence Certificate

A printed record of your registered address and personal details. Needed constantly for paperwork throughout your time in Japan.

|How to Obtain|Notes|
|---|---|
|Ward office counter|Standard, requires ID|
|Convenience store kiosk|Requires My Number Card — available 24/7 at 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson|
|Mynaportal (online)|PDF — but some institutions don't accept it; a stamped paper version may be required|

**Cost:** ¥300 per copy (convenience store) or ¥300–500 at ward office

> ⚠️ Most certificates expire within **3 months** — obtain them fresh when needed for applications.

## Address Change Notifications

When you move apartments within Japan:

- Submit a **転出届 (tenshutsutodoke)** — move-out notice — at your current ward office
- Submit a **転入届 (tenyuutodoke)** — move-in notice — at the new ward office within 14 days

## Checklist

- [ ] Register address at ward office within 14 days of arrival
- [ ] Receive My Number notification card (mailed ~1–2 months after registration)
- [ ] Apply for My Number Card (plastic IC card)
- [ ] Link My Number Card to health insurance (via hospital kiosk, Mynaportal, or 7-Bank ATM)
- [ ] Obtain a copy of Juminhyo for bank account and other applications
