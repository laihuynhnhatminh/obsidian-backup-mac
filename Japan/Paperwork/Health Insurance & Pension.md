## The Rule: Both Are Mandatory

All foreign residents in Japan staying **3+ months** are legally required to enroll in **both** the public health insurance system and the pension system. This is not optional — and gaps in payment will affect future visa renewals and permanent residency applications.

---

## Health Insurance (健康保険)

### If Employed Full-Time (Company Sponsor)

- You'll be automatically enrolled in **Employee Health Insurance (社会保険 / 健康保険)**
- Your employer handles the paperwork — just provide your residence card to HR
- Premiums are split ~50/50 between you and your employer and deducted from payroll
- Coverage: **70% of most medical costs** — you pay 30% at the point of care

### If Between Jobs or Self-Employed

- Must enroll in **National Health Insurance (国民健康保険 / NHI)** at your ward office
- Premiums are based on your **previous year's income** — first year in Japan is often low
- Apply within **14 days** of losing employer coverage — gaps in enrollment are a problem
- NHI is managed at the ward level; bring your residence card and passport

### Health Insurance Card (保険証 / Hokenshow)

- As of **December 2, 2024**: Traditional standalone health insurance cards are being phased out
- Your health insurance is now linked to your **My Number Card (マイナ保険証)**
- Register at a hospital/pharmacy kiosk, the Mynaportal website, or a 7-Bank ATM
- Always present your card (or My Number Card) when visiting a clinic or hospital

## Pension (年金)

### National Pension System (国民年金)

- For everyone aged 20–59 residing in Japan for 3+ months
- If employed full-time: automatically enrolled in **Employees' Pension Insurance (厚生年金)** via your employer. Much better deal — employer contributes ~half
- If self-employed or between jobs: enroll in **National Pension (国民年金)** at the ward office
- Current contribution (2024): ~¥16,980/month

### Why You Should Pay (Even If Planning to Leave Japan)

1. **Visa renewals** — immigration checks that you're current
2. **Permanent residency** — unpaid pension is grounds for rejection
3. **Lump-sum withdrawal (脱退一時金)** — if you leave Japan without qualifying for full pension, you can claim back contributions paid. Conditions: held less than 10 years of contributions, applied within 2 years of leaving Japan
4. **Social Security Agreement** — Japan has agreements with some countries to avoid double-paying; check if Vietnam qualifies

## Key Documents to Keep

|Document|Why You Need It|
|---|---|
|健康保険証 / Myna Insurance Card|Present at every clinic/hospital visit|
|年金手帳 (Nenkin Techo)|Pension record book — keep safe (may be digital now)|
|納付証明書|Insurance premium payment certificate — needed for PR application|
|ねんきんネット printout|Pension payment history — needed for visa renewal and PR|

---

## ねんきんネット (Nenkin Net)

Japan's official online portal for tracking your pension payments.

- Register at: https://www.nenkin.go.jp/n_net/
- Allows you to check your payment history, enrollment periods, and estimated future pension
- Required for permanent residency application as proof of contributions
