## Overview

Japan has two main taxes relevant to you as a foreign resident employee:

1. **Income Tax (所得税 / Shotokuzei)** — national tax, withheld from salary monthly
2. **Residence Tax (住民税 / Juminzei)** — local tax, based on previous year's income

If you're a full-time company employee, most of this is handled **automatically by your employer** through payroll (源泉徴収 / gensen choushu). But there are situations where you need to file yourself.

---

## Income Tax (所得税)

### If Employed Full-Time:

- Your employer withholds income tax monthly from your salary
- At year-end (December), your employer does a **year-end tax adjustment (年末調整 / nenmatsu chousei)** — this settles any overpayment/underpayment
- In most cases, you **don't need to file a tax return**

### When You Must File (確定申告):

- You have **side income over ¥200,000/year** (e.g., freelance, investments)
- You worked for **multiple employers** in the same year
- You want to claim deductions not covered by year-end adjustment (e.g., medical expenses over ¥100,000)
- You left a job mid-year and your new employer didn't do the year-end adjustment

### Filing Period:

- **February 16 – March 15** each year (for previous year's income)
- File at the local tax office (税務署 / zeimusho) or via **e-Tax (online)**

---

## Residence Tax (住民税)

- A local tax paid to the city/ward where you lived on **January 1st** of each year
- Based on the **previous year's income**
- **Not withheld from salary initially** — in your first year in Japan, you will receive a tax bill around June for the previous year's income
- After the first year, employers typically switch to **special collection (特別徴収)** where it's deducted from your monthly salary automatically

### First Year Surprise:

If you arrived partway through the year, you might receive a residence tax bill (¥100,000–¥300,000 depending on salary) in June the following year. **Budget for this.**

---

## Withholding Slip (源泉徴収票 / Gensen Choushu Hyo)

Your employer issues this at the end of each year — it's a summary of your total income and tax withheld. **Keep every one of these.** Needed for:

- Filing tax returns
- Visa renewals
- Loan or lease applications
- Permanent residency applications

---

## Tax Compliance and Immigration

Japan's immigration authority (ISA) checks tax payment compliance for:

- **Visa renewals** — late or unpaid taxes can result in shorter visa periods
- **Permanent residency** — tax delinquency is a common reason for rejection

> 💡 Set up your tax payments on **automatic bank transfer** wherever possible to avoid accidental late payments.

## Checklist

- [ ] Confirm employer handles 年末調整 at year-end
- [ ] Keep all 源泉徴収票 slips safe
- [ ] Budget for first-year 住民税 bill (arrives ~June)
- [ ] Register with e-Tax if you have additional income to declare
- [ ] Check tax compliance is current before any visa renewal
