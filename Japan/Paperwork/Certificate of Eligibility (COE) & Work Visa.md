## Overview

For most foreign workers coming to Japan, the process follows this order:

> **Job Offer → COE Application (by employer) → Visa at Embassy → Arrive in Japan → Residence Card**

This is separate from the visa itself — the COE is a **pre-approval** that your intended work and status are legally valid.

---

## Step 1: Secure a Job Offer

- Get a **written job offer and signed employment contract (労働条件通知書)**
- Confirm the role qualifies for a work visa — for tech workers, this is typically **Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services (技術・人文知識・国際業務)**
- Clarify salary, job title, and start date upfront — these go directly into the COE application

---

## Step 2: COE Application (Filed by Your Employer in Japan)

> ⚠️ **You cannot apply for your own COE from abroad.** The Japanese employer (or an immigration lawyer / 行政書士 they hire) files on your behalf at the local Regional Immigration Bureau.

### Documents Typically Required from You:

- [ ] Passport copy
- [ ] Passport-style photo (4cm × 3cm, plain white background)
- [ ] University degree certificate + transcripts (proving qualification for the visa category)
- [ ] CV / resume showing relevant work experience

### Documents the Employer Provides:

- Company registration certificate (登記簿謄本)
- Company financial statements
- Employment contract / offer letter
- Explanation of the role and how it matches the visa category

### Timeline:

- Processing: typically **1–3 months** from submission
- Result: you receive the COE document either by post (from your employer) or electronically

---

## Step 3: Visa Application at Japanese Embassy (Vietnam)

Once you have the COE, apply at the **Japanese Embassy in Hanoi or Consulate in Ho Chi Minh City**.

### Required Documents:

- [ ] COE (original + copy)
- [ ] Valid passport
- [ ] Visa application form
- [ ] Passport-style photo
- [ ] Additional documents may be requested

### Timeline:

- Usually **5–7 business days** once submitted with COE

---

## Step 4: Arrival in Japan → Residence Card

- At the airport immigration counter, present your passport, visa, and COE
- Your **Residence Card (在留カード)** is issued on the spot at major airports (Narita, Haneda, New Chitose for Hokkaido)
- **Carry this card at all times** — legally required. Failure to carry can result in a fine up to ¥200,000

---

## Visa Category Notes

### Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services

- The standard category for software engineers, IT workers, and most white-collar foreign hires
- Requires: university degree in a relevant field, OR 10+ years of equivalent work experience
- Salary must be **at least equivalent to a Japanese national** in the same role

### Highly Skilled Professional (高度専門職)

- Points-based system — 70+ points qualifies
- Points from: age, education, salary, work experience, Japanese ability, etc.
- Faster pathway to permanent residency (as little as 3 years with 70pts, 1 year with 80pts)
- Worth checking your points: https://www.immi-moj.go.jp/visa/hight_talent_calculate.html

---

## Renewal

- Work visas are typically issued for **1, 3, or 5 years** depending on the employer and your profile
- Renew at the Regional Immigration Bureau in Sapporo before expiration
- Required: passport, residence card, renewal form, photos, employment certificate, tax compliance documents

---

## Key Contact: Sapporo Regional Immigration Services Bureau

- **札幌出入国在留管理局**
- Address: Sapporo (confirm current address on the official site)
- Website: https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/
