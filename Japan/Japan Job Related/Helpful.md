They also have recruiting services to search for you, like Geekly Agent: [https://www.geekly.co.jp/content/56by6](https://www.geekly.co.jp/content/56by6 "https://www.geekly.co.jp/content/56by6") Or Findy 転職サポート: [https://findy-code.io/us-support](https://findy-code.io/us-support "https://findy-code.io/us-support") 

Or use other services, which sends you positions via mail each day to look into anything. It might take time to get the initial scouts but you can work normally and see if somethings pop up if you do not have time to check it out yourself. 

Of course changing jobs takes time, it took me over half a year from start to finish and even if I started in October the first real conversations started in Dec/January and most of the interviews in February and I was active looking. So don't think to much, oh I have no way to do interviews or so, think about how to market yourself to get a position. Especially if you do already have an employment and want to change to a better position. 

After a few months of working at a project keep in mind to not forget to update the projects and technologies you used on each site to show you are active. 

Keep a Google sheet with your CV (履歴書, rirekisho), work experience (職務経歴書, shokumu keirekisho) and skill sheet (スキルシート) updated every few months with what you did, so in case a recruiter might ask for them and you don't need to update it and can just export a PDF and attach to their mail/message they sent.

Get a template from here and change it as needed: [https://doda.jp/guide/rireki/template/](https://doda.jp/guide/rireki/template/ "https://doda.jp/guide/rireki/template/") Actual sample ideas: [https://www.r-agent.com/guide/article3151/](https://www.r-agent.com/guide/article3151/ "https://www.r-agent.com/guide/article3151/") What is needed, your name, birthday, gender, address, phone number, email. I think you don't need a 連絡先 and I would delete it if you have more job experience. Then in the next section comes your last school (often starting from high school and than if university add it) And below it with one row space added comes your work experiences. Usually only full time work but depending on what kind of work you did some アルバイト is also not a problem to add. Next section is your licenses. So if you have a Japanese driving license or other certificates add it here. Then will be often like a short paragraph what your strengths are and what makes you special. But that can be really simple but also more specific to the company you are applying to. If you have a separate 職務経歴書 this is totally fine to be empty. Depending on the template you use you can add how long it takes you to get to the company, if you have dependents, are married and the person is your dependent. And some templates also have a bigger field where you can write your desired salary or other things you want to address.
