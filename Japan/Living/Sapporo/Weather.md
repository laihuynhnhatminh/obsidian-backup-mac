## Overview

Sapporo sits in the **humid continental climate zone** — think Chicago or Toronto, but with far more snow. Annual average temperature is around **6.5°C**, with January lows hitting **-4.9°C** (and wind chill making it feel much colder). It receives approximately **483 cm of snow per year**, making it one of the snowiest major cities in the world.

## Seasonal Breakdown

|Season|Months|What to Expect|
|---|---|---|
|Spring|Apr – May|Snow melts, cool and pleasant. Cherry blossoms mid-to-late May|
|Summer|Jun – Sep|Warm, 20–28°C. Low humidity vs rest of Japan. Very comfortable|
|Autumn|Sep – Oct|Beautiful foliage, dropping fast to near 0°C by October|
|Winter|Nov – Mar|Heavy snowfall, temperatures regularly below 0°C. Can hit -10°C or lower|

## Key Challenges

### 🧊 Ice on Sidewalks

Fresh snow is walkable, but after days of compression and refreezing it turns into a sheet of ice. This is **the #1 injury risk** for newcomers.

**How to deal:**

- Buy proper **snow boots with non-slip soles** — fashion shoes are a recipe for disaster
- Walk with **small steps, flat-footed**, pressing your whole foot down
- Use **underground walkways (地下街 / chika-gai)** connecting Sapporo Station to Susukino on bad days
- Avoid rushing — slow and deliberate wins

### 🌡️ Extreme Cold

Wind chill can make -5°C feel like -15°C or worse.

**How to deal:**

- **Layer up**: thermal base (Uniqlo HeatTech is widely available), mid-layer, waterproof outer
- **Gloves, hat, scarf** — non-negotiable from November onwards
- Carry **kairo (使い捨てカイロ)** — disposable hand warmers, sold everywhere at convenience stores
- **Remove thick layers inside** buildings and trains — indoors is extremely well-heated

### 🚌 Transport Disruption

Buses can be unreliable during heavy snowstorms.

**How to deal:**

- Use the **subway** as the primary city transport in winter — it's underground and unaffected by snow
- Check forecasts before heading out (`tenki.jp` is a good app)
- Allow extra commute time from December through February

### 🏠 Home Heating Bills

Sapporo homes are well-insulated, but heating costs spike in winter.

**How to deal:**

- Expect **gas/heating bills of ¥15,000–¥25,000/month** in peak winter months
- Use thick curtains to reduce heat loss from windows
- Check if your apartment has **double-pane windows (複層ガラス)** — a major quality-of-life difference

## Condensation (結露 / ketsuro)

A major side effect of cold winters: warm air inside hits cold windows and walls → water droplets everywhere → mold potential.

**How to deal:**

- Use a **結露取りワイパー (ketsuro tori waipaa)** — a rubber wiper that collects condensation from windows each morning
- Wipe window sills and frames regularly
- Keep furniture a few centimeters from walls for airflow
