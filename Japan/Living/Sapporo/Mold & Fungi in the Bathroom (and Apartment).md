## Why It's a Big Deal

Mold is relentless in Japanese apartments — especially in summer during the rainy season (梅雨 / tsuyu, June–July) and even in Sapporo winters due to condensation from heating. Even a clean apartment will get mold if you don't actively manage humidity and ventilation. The mold is typically **black** and can spread fast overnight in warm, damp conditions.

> **Note for Sapporo specifically:** Winters bring heavy condensation on windows and walls due to the cold-outside/heated-inside contrast. Mold in Sapporo is a **year-round issue**, not just a summer one.

## High-Risk Zones

- Bathroom tiles, grout, and drain areas
- Behind furniture placed against walls
- Window sills and wall corners
- Washing machine drum interior
- Air conditioner filters
- Under/behind the toilet
- Closets and storage with poor airflow

---

## Prevention Strategies

### 🚿 Bathroom

- **Run the ventilation fan 24 hours a day** — yes, even when not in use. The cost is less than ¥500/week and absolutely worth it
- After showering, keep the bathroom door **closed** to trap the fan's effect and prevent moisture escaping to the rest of the apartment
- Wipe down walls and floor after bathing — a small squeegee helps
- Use **防カビくん煙剤 (Boukabi Kunenzai)** — a bathroom antimold fogger that uses silver particles to coat the entire room. Use every 2–3 months
- Clean the drain regularly; hair clogs trap moisture and bacteria

### 🏠 General Apartment

- **Keep air circulating**: open windows on dry days, use the A/C ventilation mode (送風 / soufuu or 換気 / kanki button) rather than full cooling to save electricity
- Keep furniture **a few centimeters away from walls** — dead air pockets behind furniture are prime mold spots
- Check **behind wardrobes and drawers** regularly — you may not notice mold there until it's bad
- Don't pile boxes or items on closet floors
- If you dry laundry indoors (which you will in winter), use detergent labeled **部屋干し洗剤用 (heya boshi senzai-yō)** — it's formulated to prevent musty smells and contains antibacterial agents

### 💧 Condensation Management

- Use a **結露取りワイパー** (window condensation wiper) every morning during winter
- Wipe window frames and sills dry — mold loves sitting water

## Cleaning Mold: Products

|Product (Japanese)|What It Does|
|---|---|
|**カビキラー (Kabi Kiraa)**|Bleach-based mold killer spray — the go-to for bathroom tiles and grout. Wear gloves and mask, open windows|
|**防カビくん煙剤**|Preventative fogger for bathroom — use after a deep clean|
|**酢 (Su / Vinegar)**|Natural alternative — spray, leave 1 hour, wipe off. Less aggressive|
|**重曹 (Juusou / Baking Soda)**|Mild scrubbing agent, good combined with vinegar|

> ⚠️ **Kabi Kiraa has bleach** — never mix with acidic cleaners. Wear gloves, a mask, and ventilate well.

## If Mold Has Already Appeared

1. **Don't ignore it** — mold permanently damages materials if left
2. Small/fresh mold: leave item in direct sunlight if possible
3. Bathroom/tile mold: spray Kabi Kiraa, wait 10–15 mins, scrub with a stiff brush, rinse
4. Check for mold **behind** the affected area — sometimes it's worse than the visible surface
5. Persistent wall mold: wipe dry, ventilate, consider repainting with mold-resistant paint after the area fully dries
