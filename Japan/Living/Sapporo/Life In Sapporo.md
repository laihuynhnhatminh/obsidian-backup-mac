## Getting Around

### Public Transit

- **Subway (地下鉄)**: 3 lines covering the central city — the backbone of daily commuting
- **Buses**: Extensive network but slow in snow/traffic
- **IC Card**: Get a **SAPICA** card (Sapporo's IC card) or use **Suica** (nationally compatible) for seamless transit payments
- The city is very walkable in spring/summer; rely on the subway in winter

### Cycling

- Very popular in spring through autumn
- **Not recommended in winter** — roads are icy and dangerous
- Some apartment buildings have bike parking; check before buying a bike

### Underground Passages (地下街)

- Poles Town and Aurora Town connect Sapporo Station to Odori and Susukino underground
- Invaluable during snowstorms — learn these routes early

### What's Special About Hokkaido Food

- **Miso ramen** — Sapporo style is rich, hearty, and warming
- **Seafood**: uni (sea urchin), ikura, crab, scallops — exceptional quality
- **Dairy**: Hokkaido milk, cheese, and butter are famous nationwide
- **Soup curry**: a Sapporo specialty — don't miss it

### Grocery Tips

- Most supermarkets mark down perishables heavily **after 7–8pm**
- Bring your own bag (エコバッグ) — plastic bags cost extra
- Learn to read **賞味期限 (shoumikigen)** = best before date, **消費期限 (shouhikigen)** = use by date

## Healthcare

- Register with a local clinic (**かかりつけ医 / kakari tsuke i**) early — having a regular doctor matters
- For emergencies: call **119** (ambulance)
- Urgent care clinics (**急患センター**) exist for after-hours non-emergency needs
- National Health Insurance covers **70% of most medical costs** — you pay 30%
- Prescriptions are filled at pharmacies (**薬局 / yakkyoku**) after a clinic visit

## Banking & Money

- **Japan Post Bank (ゆうちょ銀行)** is easiest to open as a new resident — foreign-friendly
- **7-Bank ATMs** (inside 7-Eleven) accept foreign cards and are open 24/7
- Set up **Wise** for international remittances home — significantly cheaper than bank transfers
- Most places in Sapporo still prefer **cash (現金)** — always keep some on hand
- IC cards (SAPICA/Suica) function as e-money for convenience stores and small purchases

---

## Language

- Sapporo is more foreigner-friendly than rural Hokkaido, but **English is limited** outside tourist areas
- Translation apps (Google Translate's camera mode) are essential for menus and signage
- **Pointing and gesturing** works fine at most small restaurants
- Staff will often hand you a phone or printed sheet if they can't explain something verbally
