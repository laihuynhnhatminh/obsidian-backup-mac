## The Reality Check

Even in a spotless apartment in a brand-new building, bugs will visit. Japan's climate — especially the warm, humid summer months (June–September) — creates perfect conditions for pests. Sapporo has milder bug seasons than Tokyo or Osaka, but it's not immune, especially from June through September.

> **Good news:** Japan has an excellent market for pest control products. Every drugstore (ドラッグストア) carries exactly what you need.

---

## Common Culprits

### 🪳 Gokiburi (ゴキブリ) — Cockroaches

The most notorious. Japanese cockroaches can be 25–35mm long, move fast, and are tougher than the Western varieties. They're attracted to warmth, moisture, and food — but will show up in clean homes too, especially when you first move in and disturb their hiding spots.

**Prevention:**

- Keep the kitchen clean — no food remnants left out, sealed garbage bags for organic waste
- Plug small gaps: under the front door, around pipes, drain covers, ventilation fans. A fully grown roach can squeeze into a **5mm gap**
- Don't let cardboard boxes pile up — roaches love them as shelter
- Keep your apartment cool and dry when possible

**Products:**

|Product|Type|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**ゴキブリホイホイ** (Gokiburi Hoi Hoi)|Sticky trap|Classic. Place under sink, behind appliances|
|**ブラックキャップ** (Black Cap)|Poison bait|Roaches carry it back to the nest — wipes out the colony|
|**ゴキジェットPRO** (Goki Jet Pro)|Spray|Emergency use when you spot one|

> ⚠️ **Never squash a cockroach** — if it's carrying eggs you could scatter them. Use spray or traps only.

---

### 🦟 Ka (蚊) — Mosquitoes

Active June–September. More of an annoyance than a danger in Hokkaido, but still disruptive.

**Prevention:**

- Keep windows and doors closed at night when lights are on
- Use **虫コナーズ** (mushi konazu) repellent strips hung near windows/doors
- Burn **蚊取り線香 (katorisenko)** coils on the balcony in evenings
- **アース渦巻き** or plug-in electric repellents work well indoors

---

### 🐛 Gejigeji (ゲジゲジ) — House Centipede

Looks horrifying — fast, long, many legs. But it's **harmless and non-aggressive**, and it actually hunts cockroaches, spiders, and other insects in your home.

**What to do:** If you can stomach it, leave them be. They're doing you a favor. If you want them out, seal the damp cracks and gaps they come through — they need humid environments to survive.

---

### 🦟 Mukade (ムカデ) — Giant Centipede

Unlike the Gejigeji, this one is **venomous and will bite**. More common in rural areas and ground-floor apartments near gardens. Much larger (5–15cm), reddish-brown.

**Prevention:**

- Sprinkle **ムカデコロリ (Mukade Korori)** powder around entry points
- Keep spaces dry — they seek out humidity
- Shake out shoes left near the entrance

**If bitten:** Wash the wound with soap and water immediately, apply cold pack. Seek a clinic if swelling is severe. Anti-venom is available at clinics and police boxes (交番).

---

### 🪲 Dani (ダニ) — Dust Mites

Invisible but they cause allergies, skin irritation, and itching. Live in tatami, futons, carpets, and bedding.

**Prevention:**

- Vacuum tatami and carpets regularly (at least weekly)
- Air out bedding often — sun exposure kills mites
- Consider a **布団乾燥機 (futon kansouki)** — a futon dryer, very common in Japan and very effective
- If sleeping on a futon directly on the floor, use **moisture-absorbing pads (除湿シート)** underneath

---

## Natural Repellents (if you prefer chemical-free)

- **Mint / peppermint plants** on the balcony → repels cockroaches
- **Citronella / lavender** → repels centipedes and mosquitoes
- **Dried bay leaves** in rice containers → prevents mite infestation

- [ ] Set Black Cap poison bait discs in kitchen and bathroom before furniture goes in
- [ ] Place Gokiburi Hoi Hoi traps under sink and behind fridge
- [ ] Check and seal gaps around pipes and under doors
- [ ] Buy Mukade Korori powder if ground floor or near garden
- [ ] Stock one can of Goki Jet Pro for emergencies
