## Why This Matters More Than Back Home

Japanese apartments are small, ventilation is limited, and humidity fluctuates dramatically across seasons. Neglecting cleaning leads to mold, pests, and potentially being charged for damage when you move out (退去費用). Being systematic about it makes life much easier.

Also important: **trash collection rules in Japan are strict**. Putting out the wrong type of garbage on the wrong day can result in your bag being left behind or a note from the building manager.

## Weekly Cleaning Basics

|Area|Task|Why|
|---|---|---|
|**Kitchen**|Wipe stovetop, clean drain daily|Oil and food residue attracts pests|
|**Bathroom**|Scrub tub, floor, and walls; clear drain|Mold and bacteria buildup is fast|
|**Toilet**|Clean bowl and wipe exterior|Pink bacteria (not just mold) builds fast|
|**Floors**|Vacuum, then damp mop|Dust mites and allergens|
|**Windows/sills**|Wipe condensation and sills|Mold prevention|
|**A/C filter**|Check monthly, clean every 1–2 months|Efficiency and mold prevention|

---

## Bathroom Deep Clean

1. Spray **カビキラー** on tiles, grout, drain edges — wait 10–15 mins
2. Scrub with a stiff brush
3. Rinse thoroughly
4. Wipe everything dry
5. Run the ventilation fan to finish drying
6. Use **防カビくん煙剤** fogger every 2–3 months after a deep clean

> Pink stains in the bath or sink are **bacteria (Serratia marcescens)**, not just soap scum. Use bleach to kill it, not just scrubbing.

## Kitchen: The Critical Zone

- Clean the **range hood filter** monthly — grease accumulates fast
- Don't leave dishes soaking overnight — attracts cockroaches
- Dispose of food waste in **sealed bags** before putting in the trash
- Pour boiling water down the sink drain weekly to deter pests
- Wipe the inside of the microwave and fridge regularly

---

## Air Conditioner

- Remove the front panel and pull out the filters
- Tap them outside or vacuum them — do this monthly during heavy use seasons
- Spray the filter with water if dusty, let dry fully before replacing
- Once a year, consider a deeper clean with **エアコン洗浄スプレー (A/C cleaning spray)**

## Trash Rules (ゴミ出し)

Japan has strict garbage sorting. Each municipality has its own rules. In Sapporo, common categories:

|Category|Japanese|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Burnable|燃えるゴミ|Food waste, paper, cloth|
|Non-burnable|燃えないゴミ|Ceramics, small metal items|
|Recyclable|リサイクル|Cans, bottles, plastic containers (washed)|
|Cardboard|ダンボール|Bundled with string|
|Large items|粗大ゴミ|Furniture — requires pre-booking|

**Rules:**

- Put garbage out only on the **designated day and time** (usually before 8am)
- Use **designated trash bags** if required by your ward
- Cardboard must be **bundled with string**, not put in a bag
- Never leave garbage out the night before — birds and crows will tear it apart

> 🐦 **Crows in Sapporo are smart and bold** — always use a weighted net or covered bin if your building has an outdoor trash spot.

## Laundry

- Most apartments have a **balcony for hanging laundry (ベランダ)**
- In winter you'll dry indoors — use **部屋干し用洗剤 (heya boshi senzai)** to prevent musty smell
- Wash the **washing machine drum** monthly with a drum cleaner (槽クリーナー) — bacteria and mold build up inside
- Don't leave wet laundry sitting in the machine

---

## Move-Out Cleaning (退去)

Japan is serious about this. Document the apartment's condition with **photos on move-in day** to protect yourself. Normal wear is the landlord's responsibility, but:

- Mold damage caused by inadequate ventilation → you may be charged
- Stains, burns, holes → you will be charged
- Keeping the place clean reduces potential deductions from your deposit
